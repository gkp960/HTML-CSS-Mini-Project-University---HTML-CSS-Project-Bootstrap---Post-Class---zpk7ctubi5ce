Makke Di Roti

Makki ki roti is a flat unleavened bread made from corn meal (maize flour), primarily eaten in the Rajasthan, Punjab, Haryana, Uttar Pradesh, and Uttarakhand in North India & Gujarat, Maharashtra in Western India and also in Nepal. Like most rotis in the Indian subcontinent, it is baked on a tava.

Prep time

10min

Cook time

30min

Servings

4 servings

Instructions

Step 1:

In a mixing bowl, take 2 cups maize flour or fine cornmeal (200 grams) and salt to taste. Mix everything together with a spoon and Heat 1 cup water in a separate pan till it comes to a boil. Add this hot water to the flour mixture.

Step 2:

Make medium sized balls of dough, shaping them into a neat balls and flattening them slightly with your palm. Begin to gently roll with a rolling pin (belan). Sprinkle some flour. Now with the rolling pin (belan), slowly roll.

Step 3:

Gently place the makki di roti on the tawa. When one side is a little browned, flip the makki roti with the help of a spatula. When the other side gets browned, flip again. Flip for a couple of times till the makki di roti is well browned and cooked from all sides. Drizzle a bit of ghee on the sides whilst cooking the roti.

Step 4:

Drizzle a bit of ghee on the sides whilst cooking the roti.

Ingredients

Corn flour
Salt
Butter or Ghee

Tools

Griddle (Tawa)

Spoon

Spatula

Rolling Pin and board
